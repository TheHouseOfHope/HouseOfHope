### Donor Analysis
The donor pipeline is designed to improve fundraising efficiency and retention by segmenting supporters and estimating churn risk from giving behavior, campaign history, and acquisition-channel patterns; this is primarily **predictive** (segmentation + churn modeling), not causal, because it identifies who is likely to lapse rather than proving why they lapsed. Key insights from the notebook show that donor behavior features such as gift recency/frequency, primary campaign alignment, and acquisition channel are useful signals for prioritization, which can connect directly to the website through personalized donation journeys, dynamic campaign recommendations on donor-facing pages, and an internal retention dashboard that flags at-risk donors for follow-up content, email prompts, or tailored ask amounts.

### Social Media Growth Analysis
The social media pipeline helps optimize outreach strategy by identifying which post characteristics, timing windows, and promotion styles are associated with higher engagement and donation referrals; this is best classified as **causal-adjacent observational analysis** (descriptive/diagnostic), not true causal inference, because outcomes can be confounded by content mix, audience differences, and paid spend. Insights indicate that only a subset of posts drives attributed gifts, and that post format, day/hour timing, and boosted-vs-organic choices materially change engagement and referral outcomes; these findings can be integrated into the website by improving referral attribution links, prioritizing high-converting campaign creatives on landing pages, scheduling donation CTA placement to match high-response windows, and surfacing social proof blocks tied to historically stronger post patterns.

### Case Management Analysis
The case management pipeline maps resident lifecycle and operational signals (sessions, visits, plans, incidents) and layers leakage-safe **predictive** risk scoring for serious incident escalation in a forward time window—meaning it estimates who may need more attention soon, not *why* escalation happens (that would require causal study with stronger design and fewer confounders). Core website uses include an internal **priority / supervision view** that surfaces ranked risk or caseload summaries for staff-only tools, optional **high-level impact language** on public pages about how data-informed processes support safety (without exposing individual scores), and future hooks for **secure portals** where authorized users could see non-punitive triage cues tied to follow-up workflows rather than automated decisions.
