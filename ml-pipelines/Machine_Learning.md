`Donor_Analysis.ipynb` addresses the business problem of improving donor growth and allocation strategy by combining exploratory segmentation, campaign/channel performance analysis, and fundraising KPI storytelling to identify where acquisition and stewardship should be focused; this notebook is mainly descriptive and predictive-supportive (not causal proof), so it should drive website decisions such as smarter donation journey personalization, campaign content emphasis, and impact storytelling blocks that align asks with what historically performs best.

`Donor_Churn_Analysis.ipynb` solves the operational problem of identifying supporters at near-term risk of lapsing (no donation in a forward window) so teams can prioritize retention outreach under capacity constraints; this is a production predictive pipeline (time-respecting features/labels, leakage controls, cross-validation, thresholds, and risk tiers), not a causal model, and it should integrate into the website ecosystem through internal CRM/staff dashboards and workflow APIs for ranked outreach lists while keeping individual risk scores private from public pages.

`SocialMedia_Growth_Analysis.ipynb` solves the problem of improving digital engagement and referral-driven fundraising by modeling which content, timing, and promotion choices are associated with stronger engagement and donation signals; results are predictive/observational and causal-adjacent rather than causal guarantees, so they should be used to tune website-linked campaign calendars, referral attribution flows, landing page creative variants, and CTA timing, with experiments (A/B tests) used to confirm true lift before hard policy changes.

`CaseManagement_Analysis.ipynb` solves the frontline service-delivery problem of identifying residents who may need earlier support by predicting `risk_escalation_30d` (primary) and `reintegration_success_90d` (secondary), comparing heuristic vs ML approaches, and translating outputs into risk segments and staffing/intervention priorities with ethical guardrails; this is a predictive decision-support system (not causal attribution of outcomes), and website integration should be through authenticated staff tools (caseload triage queues, supervision dashboards, and outcome tracking views) with human-in-the-loop oversight, fairness monitoring, and strict protection against exposing resident-level scores publicly.
